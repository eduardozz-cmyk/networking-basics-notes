# Networking Basics — Module 1

## What is the Internet?
The internet is not owned by any individual or group.
It is a worldwide collection of interconnected networks
that cooperate to exchange information using common standards.

---

## Types of Networks

### Small Home Networks
Connect a few computers to each other and to the internet.

### SOHO (Small Office Home Office)
Allows computers in a home or remote office to connect
to a corporate network or access shared resources.

### Medium to Large Networks
Used by corporations and schools, can have many locations
with hundreds or thousands of interconnected hosts.

### World Wide Networks
The internet connects hundreds of millions of computers
worldwide — it is literally a network of networks.

---

## The Bit
Every input device (mouse, keyboard, microphone) translates
human interaction into binary code for the CPU to process.
Every output device (printer, speakers, monitor) translates
binary data back into human recognizable form.
All data inside a computer is processed and stored as binary.

---

## Network Media
Physical medium on which signals are transmitted.

| Media Type | How it works |
|---|---|
| Copper wire | Data as electrical pulses |
| Fiber optic cable | Data as light pulses |
| Electromagnetic waves | Data through air wirelessly |

---

## Types of Signals

### Electrical Signals
Data represented as electrical pulses on copper wire.

### Optical Signals
Electrical signals converted into light pulses.

### Wireless Signals
Data transmitted using infrared, microwave, or radio waves.

---

## Key Performance Concepts

### Bandwidth
The capacity of a medium to carry data.

### Throughput
The actual measure of bits transferred across
media over a given period of time.

### Latency
The amount of time including delays for data
to travel from one point to another.

> 💡 Cybersecurity relevance: High latency can indicate
> a network attack such as a DDoS or man in the middle attack

---

## Types of Data Transmission

### Volunteered Data
Explicitly shared by individuals.
Examples: social media profiles, videos, pictures, audio files.

### Observed Data
Captured by recording actions of individuals.
Examples: location data when using cell phones.

### Inferred Data
Based on analysis of volunteered or observed data.
Examples: credit scores.

> 💡 Cybersecurity relevance: All three data types are
> targets for attackers. Personal data is valuable for
> phishing, identity theft, and social engineering attacks.
